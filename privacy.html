Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Distraction Dodge: Focus Ninja ("the app", "we", "us") is designed with privacy as a core principle. We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. This page explains exactly what that means.

Information We Do Not Collect

Distraction Dodge does not collect any personal information. We do not collect:

  • Your name, email address, or any contact information
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Location data
  • Usage analytics or telemetry
  • Crash reports sent to third-party services
  • Any behavioral or gameplay data transmitted off-device

We have no accounts, no sign-up flow, and no way to identify who you are. The app works entirely without an internet connection.

Data Storage

All data generated by Distraction Dodge ‚Äî including your session history, accuracy scores, and calm meter progress ‚Äî is stored exclusively on your device using Apple's local storage APIs. This data never leaves your device. It is not synced to iCloud, our servers, or any third-party service.

You can delete all app data at any time by deleting the app from your device.

Third-Party Services

Distraction Dodge does not integrate any third-party SDKs for analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or any other purpose.

The app's marketing website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect standard web server logs (IP address, browser type, referring page) as part of normal hosting operations. Please refer to GitHub's privacy policy for details. This data is not accessible to us and is not linked to any in-app activity.

Children's Privacy

Distraction Dodge does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Because the app collects no data whatsoever, it is suitable for users of all ages from a data privacy standpoint.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever change this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify users through an app update. We do not anticipate changes given our no-data-collection approach.
